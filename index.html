Cubera

Unity/C#
UNET Multiplayer

Cubera is a third-person multiplayer shooter, where you play a cube, rolling around and shooting at you enemies. You, as the cube, have the ability to roll yoursel, just like a die, and the side you roll onto, enebles you to use special abilities and destroy your enemies. There are four different abilieties to roll during the gameplay and you need to strategically decide which abillities to roll, depending on the situation.

Cubera was created as part of the Game Design course of Lev Ledit at the Technical University of Vienna, in the timespan of 8 Months. The purpose of the course was to experience and learn Game Design from start to finish. Our task was to come up with game ideas, present them and then to prototope two of the chosen games in the first semester. After the prototypes were done, one game was chosen for final polishing and user testing, based on user centered design. You can look at the Game Design Document for Cubera by clicking the link below.

Cellview Node editor

Unity/C#
Unity Extension

cellVIEW is a Tool for Illustrative and Multi-Scale Rendering of Large Biomolecular Datasets. It is an award-winning tool that provides fast rendering of very large biological macromolecular scenes and is inspired by state-of-the-art computer graphics techniques. It is currently still under developement by the a research tem from the TU Vienna and several other collaborating parties. For more information on cellVIEW you can click the link at the end of the page.

The cellVIEW Node Editor was my project tied to my Bachelors Thesis "Visual Scripting for Animated Transitions". The Software was developement in Unity as an Unity Editor Extension. The task was to umplement an API to allow visual scripting of Animated States of the biomolecular datasets. The Animation Framework was already all set at that point of time and I devveloped a customizable plug and play Solution in form of a Node Editor with powerful animation capabilities. The user is able to customize the Nodes and their functionality and create new Nodes, by connecting Nodes together and saving the animation as a new Node. Gunsmith VR

Unity/C#
VR
UNET Multiplayer

Gunsmith VR is an asynchronous local multiplayer virtual reality game. One player, using the HTC Vive, takes the role of the shooter and needs to survive an onslaught of enemy waves. Guns are scattered throughout the map but they are limited. That's where the asynchronous part comes into play. The second player is equipped with a Leap Motion, which is able to track his hands. This player can use specific gestures to move around in the level and craft new guns for the shooter player. The destroyed enemies drop resource pods, which can be collected by the gunsmith player to create even more guns for the shooter.

This game was created as part of one task in the Virtual Reality course at the Technical University of Vienna. The timeframe for the task was two months and it was delivered on time. This game was awarded the best game award 2016 in the course of that year.

Immersive Deck Internship

C++
OpenCV

The Immersive Deck is a real-time platform, which allows multiple users to walk and interact as teams in virtual worlds. It uses state of the art tracking technology to allow position tracking in very large rooms, used to allow tracking in Virtual Reality without being constrained compared to current know technologies like the Oculus Rift and HTC Vive. For more detiled information you can click the link at the end of the page.

My participation in the developement of the Immersive Deck was in form of an internship at the TU Vienna, a partner of Illusion Walk. I was part of a small team, which was tasked to optimize the performance and accuracy of the immersive deck on several levels. My work was strongly tied to OpenCV and C++ programming and implementing new solutions to help evaluate the accuracy of the image processing and enhance performance, by implementing new algorithms and aproaches into the immersive deck pipeline.
